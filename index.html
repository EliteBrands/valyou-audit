Problem Analysis

The Foundation is Not Set Up Correctly

Good results from ads depend on good data going in. Right now, the product feed and conversion tracking have gaps that limit how well Google can optimise campaigns — no matter how much budget is spent.

06
Product feed and tracking are not optimised
Feed quality & conversion tracking — Foundation audit
Critical

Simple explanation: Google uses the product feed (titles, descriptions, images) and conversion data to decide who to show ads to. If the feed is missing keywords or has poor descriptions, Google cannot match ads to the right searches. Fixing the foundation means every dollar of ad spend works harder — without increasing budget.

📦 Product Feed — Missing Keywords
✗ Product titles are too short and missing search terms. e.g. "Feathers Sofa" should be "Feathers Modular Sectional Sofa — Cloud Couch, Luxury Living Room Furniture"
✗ Descriptions lack buying keywords. Google reads descriptions to understand what the product is and who to show it to. Generic descriptions mean generic (irrelevant) traffic.
✗ Missing product attributes. Material, dimensions, colour, and style tags help Google match products to the right searches. These fields appear incomplete.
📊 Conversion Tracking — Not Fully Utilised
✗ Add-to-cart and checkout tracked but not used. These micro-conversion signals help Google's AI find customers who are close to buying — but they are currently set as secondary and ignored by Smart Bidding.
✗ YouTube conversion actions are cluttering the account. 2 of 6 tracked conversions are YouTube-related and not relevant to purchase optimisation. They add noise to Google's bidding AI.
⚠ Only 122 purchases recorded in 30 days. Google recommends at least 30–50 conversions per month per campaign for Smart Bidding to work well. Several campaigns are below this threshold.

Why This Limits Scaling

Increasing ad budget on a poor foundation does not produce proportional results — it amplifies the existing inefficiencies. The campaigns that are struggling (zero conversions, high CPA) are partly failing because Google's AI does not have enough quality data to optimise correctly. Fixing the feed and tracking unlocks the full potential of Google's Smart Bidding and Performance Max — without spending more money.

How to Fix This
1
Rewrite product titles with search keywords. Format: [Brand] + [Product Name] + [Key Feature] + [Category] + [Style]. Example: "Valyou Feathers Sectional Sofa — Deep Seat Cloud Couch, Modular Living Room Furniture". Do this for the top 20 products by ad spend first.
2
Expand product descriptions with buyer language. Include material (fabric type, foam density), dimensions, use cases ("perfect for open-plan living rooms"), and style keywords ("minimalist", "luxury", "contemporary"). Google reads every word.
3
Complete all product attributes in the feed. Fill in: colour, material, item group ID, product type, Google product category. Missing attributes reduce eligibility for Shopping placements.
4
Remove YouTube conversion actions from the account goals. Keep only: Purchase (primary). Remove or archive: YouTube subscriptions, YouTube views, Android installs. This gives Smart Bidding a cleaner signal to optimise towards.
5
Enable add-to-cart as a campaign-level bid signal. For campaigns with fewer than 30 monthly purchases, use "add-to-cart" as the optimisation goal temporarily — this gives Google enough conversion data to bid intelligently while the purchase volume grows.
